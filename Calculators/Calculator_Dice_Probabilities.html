Dice Probability Calculators

P(d, n) = 1 - (d - 1 / d)^n

This calculates the probability of obtaining a single specific outcome across n rolls of a d-sided die.

For example, I want to find out the probability of rolling one 20 in 2d20.
I input n as 2 because we are rolling 2 20-sided dice and then I input d as 20 because each die as 20 sides. The result is a probablity of 9.75%.





P(d, n, o) = 1 - (d - (d - o + 1) / d)^n

This calculates the probability of obtaining an outcome of o or greater, on at least one of n d-sided dice.

For example, I want to find the probability of rolling a 15 or higher on 2d20.
I input n as 2 because I'm rolling 2 dice.Then, I input d as 20 because each die has 20 sides. Finally, I input o as15 because I want to roll a 15 or higher. This gives us a probability of 51%.
